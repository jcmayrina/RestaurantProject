DESSERT


The native Tagalog word for “dessert” is pang-himagas. Many Philippine fruits are cooked in syrup to serve as dessert. The Filipino has a sweet tooth, likely because sugar is abundant and cheap in the country.

Desserts are usually served right after lunch or dinner, but Filipinos are known for not adhering to the standard mealtimes(breakfast, lunch, and dinner). Instead, they have what they call "merienda" in the morning and in the mid-afternoon. Merienda is a light-meal or snack that can either be savory or sweet.
